Frequently Asked Questions

What does homeowners insurance cover?

Homeowners insurance typically covers your home, personal belongings, liability, and additional living expenses in case of covered events.

Is flood damage covered?

Standard homeowners insurance policies do not cover flood damage. Separate flood insurance is available for purchase.

How can I file a claim?

You can file a claim through our website, mobile app, or by calling our 24/7 claims service hotline.

What is liability coverage?

Liability coverage protects you financially if someone is injured on your property or if you accidentally damage someone else's property.

Are earthquakes covered?

Earthquakes are not covered under standard policies but can be added as an endorsement or separate policy.

What is replacement cost coverage?

Replacement cost coverage pays to rebuild or repair your home and replace belongings without depreciation.

Do I need homeowners insurance if I own my home outright?

While not legally required, homeowners insurance is highly recommended to protect your investment.

Can I adjust my coverage limits?

Yes, you can adjust your coverage limits by contacting us or through your online account.

Does my policy cover water damage?

Water damage from sudden events like a burst pipe is typically covered, but gradual damage or flooding may not be included.

What is a deductible?

A deductible is the amount you pay out of pocket before your insurance covers the remaining costs of a claim.

Can I bundle my homeowners insurance with other policies?

Yes, we offer discounts for bundling homeowners insurance with auto or other policies.

What is personal property coverage?

Personal property coverage protects your belongings, such as furniture, electronics, and clothing, against covered events.

What is additional living expenses (ALE) coverage?

ALE coverage helps pay for temporary housing and other living expenses if your home is uninhabitable due to a covered loss.

How do I add additional coverage for valuable items?

You can add endorsements for high-value items like jewelry, art, or collectibles to ensure they are fully covered.

Does my policy cover mold damage?

Mold damage is typically not covered unless it results from a covered event like a burst pipe.

Are home-based businesses covered?

Homeowners insurance may not cover business-related equipment or liability. Consider additional business insurance.

How often should I review my policy?

We recommend reviewing your policy annually or after significant life events to ensure it meets your needs.

What happens if my neighbor’s tree damages my home?

Your policy usually covers damage to your home regardless of who owns the tree. Check specific policy details.

What should I do after a loss?

Ensure safety, document the damage, and contact us to start the claims process.

Is wear and tear covered?

Normal wear and tear or maintenance issues are not covered under homeowners insurance.

Can I transfer my policy to a new home?

Yes, you can transfer your policy to a new home. Contact us to update your policy details.

What is an umbrella policy?

An umbrella policy provides additional liability coverage beyond the limits of your homeowners policy.
