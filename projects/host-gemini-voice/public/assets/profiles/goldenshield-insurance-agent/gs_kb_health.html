Frequently Asked Questions

What types of health insurance plans do you offer?

We offer individual, family, and group health insurance plans, including HMOs, PPOs, and high-deductible health plans (HDHPs).

What is the difference between an HMO and a PPO?

HMOs require you to choose a primary care physician and get referrals for specialists. PPOs offer more flexibility to see any doctor without referrals.

Are pre-existing conditions covered?

Yes, our plans comply with the Affordable Care Act and cover pre-existing conditions without additional charges.

What is the enrollment period?

The open enrollment period typically occurs once a year. Special enrollment periods are available for qualifying life events.

How can I find an in-network doctor?

You can use our online provider directory or contact customer service to find in-network doctors and facilities.

Do you cover mental health services?

Yes, our plans include coverage for mental health services such as therapy, counseling, and psychiatric care.

What is a deductible?

A deductible is the amount you must pay out-of-pocket before your insurance starts covering your medical expenses.

Are prescription medications covered?

Yes, our plans include prescription drug coverage. Check your plan details for the formulary and co-pay information.

What is co-insurance?

Co-insurance is the percentage of medical costs you pay after meeting your deductible. For example, an 80/20 plan means we cover 80%, and you cover 20%.

What is a co-payment?

A co-payment is a fixed amount you pay for certain services, such as doctor visits or prescriptions, at the time of service.

Can I add dependents to my plan?

Yes, you can add dependents to your plan during the enrollment period or after a qualifying life event.

What is out-of-pocket maximum?

The out-of-pocket maximum is the most you will pay for covered services in a year. Once you reach it, we cover 100% of eligible expenses.

Do you offer telehealth services?

Yes, telehealth services are included in most plans, allowing you to consult with doctors remotely.

What is an Explanation of Benefits (EOB)?

An EOB is a statement summarizing the costs your insurance covered and what you owe after a medical service.

Are maternity services covered?

Yes, our plans include maternity and newborn care as essential health benefits.

How can I check the status of a claim?

You can check the status of a claim through our online portal or by contacting our customer service team.

Do you cover preventive care?

Yes, preventive services such as vaccinations, screenings, and annual check-ups are fully covered under most plans.

Can I change my plan?

You can change your plan during the open enrollment period or after a qualifying life event.

Do you cover alternative therapies?

Coverage for alternative therapies like acupuncture or chiropractic care depends on the specific plan. Check your policy for details.

What should I do in case of an emergency?

In an emergency, seek care immediately at the nearest facility. Emergency services are covered regardless of network status.

How do I appeal a denied claim?

You can appeal a denied claim by following the instructions provided in your Explanation of Benefits (EOB).
