Frequently Asked Questions

What types of dental plans do you offer?

We offer individual, family, and group dental plans with varying levels of coverage, including preventive, basic, and major services.

Are routine cleanings covered?

Yes, routine cleanings are fully covered under most of our plans, typically twice a year.

How can I find a network dentist?

You can search for a network dentist using our online directory or by contacting our customer support team for assistance.

Do you cover orthodontic treatments?

Some of our plans include coverage for orthodontic treatments, such as braces or aligners. Please review your plan details for specific coverage.

What happens if I visit an out-of-network dentist?

Visiting an out-of-network dentist may result in higher out-of-pocket costs. Check your plan for specific out-of-network coverage details.

Are dental implants covered?

Certain plans offer partial coverage for dental implants. Coverage details depend on the specific plan you choose.

How do I file a claim?

Most in-network dentists file claims on your behalf. For out-of-network services, you may need to submit a claim form along with receipts and documentation.

What is the waiting period for major procedures?

Waiting periods for major procedures vary by plan and typically range from 6 to 12 months.

Can I add my dependents to my plan?

Yes, you can add dependents to your plan during open enrollment or within 30 days of a qualifying life event.

Do you offer coverage for cosmetic dentistry?

Cosmetic procedures, such as teeth whitening, are generally not covered by our plans. Check your policy for specific details.

What is the annual maximum benefit?

The annual maximum benefit varies by plan, typically ranging from $1,000 to $3,000.

Do plans cover emergency dental treatments?

Yes, most plans cover emergency treatments, such as tooth extractions and immediate care for dental injuries.

How do I estimate the cost of dental services?

You can use our online cost estimator to get a breakdown of expenses based on your plan and treatment type.

Are pediatric dental services covered?

Yes, our family plans include coverage for pediatric dental care, including checkups, fluoride treatments, and sealants.

Can I upgrade my dental plan?

You can upgrade your plan during open enrollment or if a qualifying life event occurs.

Is sedation dentistry covered?

Sedation dentistry for major procedures is covered under some plans. Please review your policy for more information.

Do plans include coverage for crowns and bridges?

Yes, crowns and bridges are typically included under major services, subject to your plan's deductible and coinsurance.

What happens if I miss a premium payment?

If a payment is missed, you have a grace period to make it before coverage lapses. Review your policy for specific timelines.

Are dental X-rays covered?

Most plans cover routine and diagnostic X-rays as part of preventive or basic services.

Can I cancel my dental plan at any time?

Yes, but cancellation terms depend on whether it's during the policy period or an open enrollment window.

What dental services are excluded?

Excluded services may include elective cosmetic treatments and experimental procedures. Refer to your policy for a detailed list.

What is the difference between in-network and out-of-network coverage?

In-network dentists agree to negotiated rates, reducing out-of-pocket costs. Out-of-network coverage may involve higher costs.
